# Impeccable — Design Context

Project: Arsen's portfolio (Vue 3 + TS + SCSS/BEM). Most of the site is a dark,
red-accented (`--c-primary: #fe0000`) showcase. The **/services** area is a
distinct light "instrument" world (see below) — a deliberate, contextual break.

## Services / capital calculator — "Capital Instrument"

### Users
Portfolio clients and visitors evaluating Arsen's craft, plus people who want a
free, serious finance tool. Browsed in daylight, decision-making mode.

### Brand Personality
Precise · trustworthy · "adult money". No infotainment, no hype.

### Aesthetic Direction — light precision financial terminal
- **Theme:** light. Warm paper, never pure white/black. Tinted toward the brand red hue (~40° in OKLCH).
- **Signal colour:** the brand red `#fe0000` is the ONLY accent (≈10% weight) — primary chart line, key figures, live dot, active states, section numbers. Everything else is ink/graphite.
- **Secondary data colour:** cool graphite/slate for the "today's prices" series.
- **Structure:** hairline rules and framed grids, NOT shadowed/rounded cards. Sharp 2–3px radii. Flat — no glow, no blur, no glassmorphism.
- **Type:** Clash Display (display + big tabular figures) · Geist (prose) · Geist Mono (data, labels, tabular numerals). Numbers use `font-feature-settings: 'tnum' 1`.
- **Readout, not metric cards:** one large primary figure + a hairline-divided strip. The 4-identical-cards template is banned here.
- **Catalog = editorial directory:** numbered hairline rows (N°01…), not a card grid.

### Tokens
Defined under `.instrument` in `src/assets/styles/app.scss` (`--cap-*`): paper,
panel, ink/ink-2/ink-3, rule/rule-strong, red/red-deep, slate, and the three font
stacks (`--cap-display/-sans/-mono`). Child components inherit them.

### Design Principles
1. One signal colour. Red earns its weight by being rare.
2. Hairlines over boxes. Structure with rules and grids, not shadows.
3. Tabular figures everywhere numbers live; align the money.
4. Flat and precise — if it glows or blurs, it's wrong here.
5. Light by intent (daylight financial tool), even though the rest of the site is dark.
